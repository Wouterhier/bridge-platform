# SelfCareMen Knowledge Base (Stub)

SelfCareMen is a New Zealand men's telehealth clinic. We provide discreet, professional consultations for men's health concerns including testosterone replacement therapy (TRT), erectile dysfunction (ED), weight management, performance-enhancing drug recovery, and nutritional coaching.

## Services

- **Free Eligibility Consultation** — 15 min, free. Determine which clinical pathway fits the patient. This is a no-obligation conversation with a clinician to understand symptoms, goals, and medical history. No prescription or treatment is provided during this call.

- **TRT Initial Consultation** — 30 min, $179. For patients seeking testosterone replacement therapy. Includes review of symptoms, baseline blood results, medical history, and discussion of treatment options if appropriate. A follow-up blood test is usually required before treatment begins.

- **TRT Follow-up** — 20 min, $159. Ongoing care for established TRT patients. Reviews recent blood work, symptom changes, dosage adjustments, and side effects.

- **TRT On-Treatment Follow-up** — 20 min, $159. For patients already on TRT who need a routine check-in. Focuses on monitoring testosterone levels, hematocrit, estrogen, and prostate markers.

- **TRT Express Follow-up** — 15 min, $99. A brief check-in for stable patients with no recent dose changes or concerns.

- **ED Initial Consultation** — 30 min, $179. Erectile dysfunction assessment. Discusses underlying causes, cardiovascular risk, medication options, and lifestyle factors. May require blood tests or GP referral depending on findings.

- **GLP-1 Initial Consultation** — 20 min, $119. Weight-loss peptide pathway. Reviews BMI, medical history, current medications, and suitability for GLP-1 receptor agonists.

- **GLP-1 Follow-up** — 15 min, $99. Monitoring progress, side effects, dosage, and adherence for GLP-1 patients.

- **RoidCare+ Initial** — 30 min, $179. Specialized care for patients using or recovering from anabolic steroids, SARMs, or performance-enhancing drugs. Non-judgmental assessment of current or past use, blood work, fertility concerns, cardiovascular risk, and mental health.

- **RoidCare+ Follow-up** — 20 min, $169. Ongoing monitoring for RoidCare+ patients, including blood work review and adjustment of recovery protocols.

- **Nutrition Initial** — 30 min, $159. Nutrition coaching introductory session. Reviews diet, goals, medical conditions, and builds an initial plan.

- **Nutrition Follow-up** — 20 min, $129. Progress review and plan adjustments.

- **Weight Management Initial** — 30 min, $159. Structured weight management program. Combines nutrition, medication review, and lifestyle planning.

- **Weight Management Follow-up** — 20 min, $129. Ongoing support and monitoring for weight management patients.

## Eligibility

- Patients must be physically located in New Zealand at the time of consultation.
- Patients must be 18 years of age or older.
- Some services require a prior consultation or being an existing patient (e.g., follow-ups, on-treatment visits, express visits).
- TRT patients must provide recent blood work before starting treatment. If blood work is not available, the clinician will order appropriate tests.
- GLP-1 and weight management services require a BMI and medical suitability assessment.
- RoidCare+ is the only service where discussion of steroid, SARM, or PED use is expected and should not trigger general escalation pathways.
- We do not prescribe anabolic steroids or SARMs for muscle-building or athletic enhancement outside of RoidCare+ recovery protocols.

## Booking

- Paid services require payment before the appointment is confirmed in our system.
- The free eligibility consultation is booked directly without payment.
- Appointments are booked through Acuity Scheduling.
- Cancellations or reschedules should be made at least 24 hours in advance.
- Consultations are conducted via video call or phone depending on patient preference and clinical need.

## Communication Style

- Be calm, precise, and warm.
- Never be salesy or pushy.
- Use plain language. Avoid medical jargon unless explaining it.
- Do not use em dashes, semicolons, or informal greetings such as "Hey".
- Do not use exclamation points in opening lines.
